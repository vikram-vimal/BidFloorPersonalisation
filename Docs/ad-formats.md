# Ad Formats

Mobile games support several ad formats through mediation. Each trades off visibility, user experience, and revenue differently.

## Banner ads

Small rectangular strips anchored to the top or bottom of the screen during active gameplay. They are always visible but non-intrusive, relying heavily on imagery due to limited space. Their passive nature makes them easy to ignore — the average click-through rate is as low as **0.06%**.

{% hint style="success" %}
**Best for:** Brand awareness, not direct conversions.
{% endhint %}

## Interstitial ads

Full-screen ads that take over the entire display, typically appearing between game levels or at loading screens. Users must wait several seconds before a close button appears. Two common formats:

- **Static** — image and text, similar to a large banner
- **Video** — a short clip plays before the close option appears

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Best for:** High visibility — but risks frustrating users if overused.
{% endhint %}

## Rewarded video ads

Short clips (30–60 seconds) that play between levels or during loading screens. The player earns an in-game bonus (coins, lives, etc.) for watching fully.

{% hint style="success" %}
**Best for:** Rewarded video is the industry's top recommended format for free-to-play games.
{% endhint %}

## Other formats

Mediation platforms also support **MREC** (medium rectangle) and **native** ads. Configure these as separate ad units with the appropriate format type in your mediation dashboard.
